Slide 2 · Key Lesson

Incomplete data is equally misleading as false data.

What I saw

Min Group Welfare = 49.6 ✓
Survival rate = 67% ✗ — a third already dead

Why it looked fine

Dead agents drop out of the average. The survivors look healthy. The metric reports the truth — just not the whole truth.

  • The rule: always ask — who is still in this average?
  • Real world: a hospital reports "average patient health = excellent" — but the sickest patients already left. Survivors skew everything.
Slide 3 · My Stance on Fairness

"What rules would you pick if you didn't know whether you'd be born Group A or Group C?"

In the sim, the Rawlsian rule protected the weakest — it lifted Group C from health 23 → 40, and raised everyone's average. That's fairness from behind the veil.

What Rawls missed

Rawls assumes equal rules = equal opportunity. But equal rules applied to unequal people isn't equal opportunity.

What Sen added

Equal rules aren't enough. The real question: what can you actually do with them?

  • Rawls gives everyone the same rules.
  • Sen asks whether those rules actually expand what the weakest person can do.
  • Fair process is the floor. Real capability is the ceiling.
